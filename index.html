ZPD Reports Record-Low Predator Crime Rate
By Chester Vole · City Desk

Chief Bogo announced yesterday that Zootopia's Predator District recorded its lowest crime rate since the city's founding, attributing the milestone to expanded community policing and Officer Hopps's innovative mediation program.

"We have come a long way," Bogo said from the podium, adjusting his reading glasses with a massive hoof. "This city proves every single day that species don't define destiny."

The program, piloted last spring, pairs predator and prey officers in joint neighbourhood patrols, fostering trust at the grassroots level.

"Try everything — and then try again. That's Zootopia."

'The Case That Nearly Broke the City' — An Exclusive with Judy & Nick
By Francine Fawn · Senior Reporter  |  Photography: ZPD Archive
🦊🐰
Det. Nick Wilde and Officer Judy Hopps outside ZPD headquarters following the press conference. The pair declined to reveal details of the new investigation. (ZPD Archive / The Zootopia Times)

It has been nearly a decade since Judy Hopps arrived at the Zootopia Police Department as its first rabbit officer, clutching a parking ticket book and an impossible dream. Today she sits across from us in a worn leather chair, detective badge gleaming, with her partner — the reformed con-fox Nick Wilde — perched on the armrest beside her, smirking in that inimitable way of his.

"The city keeps throwing surprises at us," Hopps says, her violet eyes bright. "Every time we think we've seen everything, something new walks through the precinct door."

That something new, sources close to the ZPD confirm, is a sprawling conspiracy involving a network of predator-prey alliances that have been secretly manipulating Zootopia's commodity markets. The Night Howler crisis of years past seems quaint by comparison.

"We can't say much," Wilde cuts in, straightening his tie — green, naturally. "Except that it goes high. Very high. Think penthouse-suite-in-the-Cliffside-Asylum high."

Director Confirms New Villain Is 'Unlike Anything Seen in Zootopia'
By Staff Wire · Entertainment

Disney Animation confirmed that Zootopia 2 introduces a villain whose motivations challenge the city's founding philosophy. "We wanted to write a character who genuinely believes they're saving Zootopia," director Byron Howard told reporters at a press junket held at Savanna Studios. "That's far scarier than pure evil."

The film's score, once again composed by Michael Giacchino, reportedly features a 90-piece orchestra with live recordings in three of the city's iconic climate districts.

Opinion
Why Zootopia 2 Arrives at Exactly the Right Moment
Editorial Board

Ten years on from the original, the world — both ours and theirs — has grown more complicated. The naive optimism of "anyone can be anything" has been tested by economic friction between districts, rising housing costs in Tundratown, and the ongoing debate over Rainforest resource rights.

The sequel, by all accounts, does not flinch from these tensions. If the first film used predator-prey anxiety as a metaphor for real-world prejudice, the second appears to use systemic inequality — the kind baked into neighbourhoods and institutions — as its canvas.

Zootopia needs these stories as much as we do.

Bellwether Parole Hearing Draws Protesters
By Roberta Finch · Courts

Hundreds of citizens gathered outside the Zootopia Correctional Facility on Tuesday as former Deputy Mayor Dawn Bellwether appeared before the parole board for the first time since her 2016 conviction.

The board granted no early release. Bellwether, now 44, addressed the media briefly: "I understand why the city cannot forgive yet. I am working on forgiving myself." The scene drew mixed reactions from the crowd outside.
